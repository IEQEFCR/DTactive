Abstract

The advancement of vision-based tactile sensors has greatly improved robots’ perception and manipulation capabil- ities, crucial for tasks demanding contact-rich interaction with objects. In this work, we present a novel vision-based tactile sensor with active surface, named DTactive. DTactive inherits and modifies the tactile optical principles of 9DTact and innovatively incorporates a transmission mechanism, enabling the sensor’s surface to be movable. Thanks to this design, the sensor can simultaneously possess tactile perception and the capability to actively manipulate objects, which facilitates robust object rolling manipulation. Additionally, leveraging the high- resolution tactile heightmap from the sensor and encoder data from the transmission system, we propose a non-visual method to estimate object’s orientation during in-hand rolling manipulation. In our experiments, we successfully implemented active rolling manipulation ranging from -180 to 180 degrees for 4 objects of different shapes with accurate online pose estimation. In addition, we also tested the rolling manipulation of 10 irregularly shaped objects with an average success rate of over 95%, demonstrating the significant enhancement in robustness for closed-loop object manipulation brought by tactile perception and active surfaces

Desgin and Fabrication

Gripper

A readily manufacturable two-fingered gripper, whose end effector sensors can open and close along a linear path and rotate about an axis parallel to that line.

Sensor

A vision-based tactile sensor providing high-resolution tactile images, while enabling the tactile surface to actively move to provide additional degrees of freedom.
